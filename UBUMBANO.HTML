Introduction

Gender-based violence (GBV) is a immense and extensive issue in South Africa impacting on almost every aspect of life. GBV (which unfairly affects women and girls) is systemic, and deeply entrenched in institutions, cultures and tradiontions in South Africa.


Our organistaion explores what GBV is and educates the community on GBV and all the forms it takes and also explores how to respond to such situations.

What we do

Ubumbano Re-Build Centre (URC) is a place of wellbeing for those seeking change from a lifestyle of alcohol and other drug related issues, we provide holistic residential rehabilitation and treatment followed by after in communities. We are an intensive rehabilitation program and we offer a stable, secure enviroment where young people can make positive changes.


Our Program

We assist young people to:

  • Manage problematic behaviors
  • Improve thier life skills and interpersonal skills
  • Learn alternative habits to develop personal resilience and confidence.
  • Build social networks
  • Reintegrate into the community with three months of aftercare support.
Vision and Mission

Vision

The Ubumbano Re-Build Centre aspires to be a center of excellence, to successsfully restore hope, dignity and integrity of our brothers and sisters as they too can become citizens who can make a positive contributions to the development of our country.


Mission

Our mission is to effectively restore hope to those who are already trapped in the vicious cycle of substance abuse.

  • We want to eradicate substance abuse in our communities.
  • Reduce the number of homeless people in our communities.
  • Offer motivational talks at schools and churches.
  • Increase awareness about the dangers and dire consequences of substance abuse.
